Methodology applied

Validation pipeline

The 60.5% figure at 5s isn't a raw backtest number — it survived five separate checks before being published here.

01

Walk-forward, spaced sampling

Validation windows are spaced out in time, never adjacent to training data, to avoid temporal leakage.

02

Block bootstrap

Resampled with multiple block sizes to check that the confidence interval isn't an artifact of one block length.

03

Permutation test

Targets are shuffled and the model re-scored, to confirm the edge disappears when the real signal is destroyed.

04

Gap bootstrap

The gap between real and permuted performance is bootstrapped directly to test significance of the edge itself.

05

Temporal stability

Results are split into 15-day partitions to confirm the edge holds across time, not just in one lucky window.

Honest note

Real edge, unprofitable in practice

⚠️

The 5s edge is real — but exchange fees erase it

The +8pp edge at the 5-second horizon is statistically significant: it survives walk-forward validation, block bootstrap, permutation testing, and gap bootstrap. This is not noise.

It is also economically unviable for a retail trader under standard Binance fees. Net PnL is negative across every VIP fee tier we tested at this horizon — the edge is consumed by trading costs before it becomes profit.

We're publishing this as a complete case study: the validation proves the edge exists, and the fee analysis proves why it doesn't translate into profitable retail trading. Both halves matter.
